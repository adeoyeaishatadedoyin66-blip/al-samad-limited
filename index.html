Ankara Clothe

Ankara cloth is a colorful cotton fabric known for its bold patterns, also called African wax print or Dutch wax print. Though often associated with Africa, it has Indonesian origins and was originally produced by a Dutch company for the Indonesian market before gaining widespread popularity in West Africa in the 1900s. The fabric is now a symbol of African fashion and is used for clothing, accessories, and home decor.

Lace clothes

Lace is a delicate, ornamental fabric characterized by its openwork patterns and net-like structure. It is made by weaving, knitting, crocheting, or embroidering threads from materials like cotton, silk, or synthetic fibers. Lace is used for decorative purposes in clothing, especially bridal wear and lingerie, as well as in home decor and crafts to add elegance.

Ghana Textile

Ghanaian textiles include traditional woven cloths like Kente and Adinkra (hand-printed), as well as modern products like African print fabrics and tie-dye/batik. The industry is a strategic sector for job creation and export, though it faces challenges from competition with cheap imported textiles and second-hand clothing. Despite this, there is a strong focus on "made in Ghana" products and adapting traditions to modern markets and international fashion.
